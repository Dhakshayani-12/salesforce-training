# Day 1 - CRM Basics

## What is CRM?

CRM stands for Customer Relationship Management.

It is a system used by companies to manage customer information, track communication, improve sales, and provide better customer service.

CRM helps businesses:
- Store customer data
- Track sales activities
- Improve customer relationships
- Manage business workflows efficiently

Salesforce is one of the most popular CRM platforms in the world.

---

# Why Companies Use Salesforce

Companies use Salesforce because it helps them:

- Manage customer data in one place
- Track leads and sales opportunities
- Improve communication between teams
- Automate business processes
- Increase productivity and sales
- Provide better customer support

Salesforce is cloud-based, so employees can access data from anywhere.

---

# Salesforce Core Concepts

## Account

An Account represents a company, organization, or business that a company is working with. In Salesforce, all business related information about a company is stored inside the account.

Example:
- TCS
- Infosys
- Amazon

Accounts store business information like:
- Company name
- Industry
- Location
- Revenue

---

## Contact

A Contact represents a person associated with an Account. Contacts usually include customer details such as name, email, phone number, and designation.

Example:
- HR Manager
- Customer
- Employee

Contacts store details like:
- Name
- Phone number
- Email address

---

## Opportunity

An Opportunity represents a potential sales deal or business deal that a company is trying to close. It contains information related to the product or service being sold, deal amount, and sales stage.

It helps companies track:
- Deal value
- Sales stage
- Expected revenue
- Closing date

Example:
A company discussing a software purchase with Salesforce.

---

Core Task 1: Business Flow

Lead → Contact → Opportunity → Customer

The business process in Salesforce usually starts with a Lead.
A Lead is a person who shows interest in a product or service but is not yet confirmed as a customer. For example, a person filling out a form on a company website can be considered a Lead.

Once the sales team communicates with the lead and confirms that the person is genuinely interested, the lead is converted into a Contact. A Contact contains the verified details of the person.

After that, an Opportunity is created. An Opportunity represents a possible business deal or sales deal between the company and the customer. The sales team works on the opportunity to close the deal successfully.

When the deal is completed successfully and the customer purchases the product or service, the person officially becomes a Customer.

# Core Task 2: Real-Life Mapping (College Admission Example)

Account = College
Contact = Student
Lead = Student inquiry about admission
Opportunity = Admission process or fee payment process

In a College Admission System, the College is considered as the Account because it is the organization managing admissions. The Student is treated as the Contact because the student communicates with the college.

When a student first asks about admission or fills out an inquiry form, the student is called a Lead. After the student shows genuine interest and starts the admission process, it becomes an Opportunity. The Opportunity includes activities like application submission, counseling, and fee payment.

Once the admission is completed successfully, the student becomes part of the college. This process helps colleges manage students and admissions in an organized way.
